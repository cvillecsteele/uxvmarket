*Items dated 2026-05-05 through 2026-05-11*

Last week we said watch Kratos Q1 to see if the attritable/services thesis prints. It printed. $371M revenue, 1.6 book-to-bill, FY26 guidance raised. Unmanned Systems segment called out as a growth driver; management cited demand for Valkyrie CCA, rocket motors, drone engines, and microwave electronics. Uber-for-ISR and the attritable airframe model are the same DOW play in two layers. The books just confirmed it. SITREP prediction graded: pays out.

Two days later, Kratos announced [Project Helios in Odon, Indiana](https://www.kratosdefense.com/newsroom/kratos-names-odon-indiana-as-home-of-new-hypersonic-test-facility), a mid-tier coupled arc jet and laser facility for hypersonic aerothermal testing. Read this against last week's [Element Huntsville expansion](https://www.element.com/about-element/news/2026/05/01/element-huntsville-established-as-strategic-hub-for-innovation) and a pattern shows up. Element is building external test capacity the merchant tier can rent. Kratos is building captive capacity because they don't trust external supply to scale on their program calendar. Same constraint, two answers.

Here's the new recurring bit. **Rented vs. captive.** When primes build their own test stands, the external queue isn't keeping up. That's a leading indicator of who actually thinks they have a production ramp coming.

A few signals from the supplier tier, one sentence each.

[Doodle Labs](https://doodlelabs.com/blog/drone-dominance-at-scale-why-supply-chain-matters-more-than-specs/) wrote a piece that's mostly self-promotion but published their own constraint numbers: 200K+ radios/year, week-scale lead times, sub-$1000 per radio in early phases. Vendors naming their own scaling limits is signal.

[SPH Engineering's drone-based methane detection workflow](https://www.sphengineering.com/news/peer-reviewed-drone-based-methane-detection-at-abandoned-oil-and-gas-wells) got validated in a peer-reviewed *Sensors* paper. Vendor stacks showing up in actual journals (not white papers, not press releases) is rare. Matrice 300 RTK, Pergam Laser Falcon, SkyHub, UgCS, working over abandoned wells in Houston, Alaska. (Yes, Alaska.)

[NORBIT shipped a real-time 3D point cloud viewer](https://norbit.com/newsroom/experience-norbits-real-time-3d-point-cloud-viewer) for their X-series sonar with INS integration. Maritime/subsea is the underrepresented sector on this beat. We're flagging it because we should be flagging more of it.

Bullshit detector: silent.

## https://www.kratosdefense.com/newsroom/kratos-names-odon-indiana-as-home-of-new-hypersonic-test-facility

**Date: 2026-05-08 · Odon, IN.** Kratos selected Odon, Indiana as the site for Project Helios, a mid-tier coupled arc jet and laser hypersonic test facility. The facility is intended to expand US aerothermal testing capacity for hypersonic materials and to bridge laboratory-stage materials development with operational program needs.

Aerothermal testing simulates the extreme heat and pressure environments hypersonic systems encounter in flight; coupled arc jet plus laser facilities are scarce and typically queued. Mid-tier capacity (between national-lab scale and end-customer scale) is particularly constrained. The Odon decision positions Kratos to internalize a portion of that bottleneck for its own hypersonics programs while potentially serving partners.

## https://www.sphengineering.com/news/peer-reviewed-drone-based-methane-detection-at-abandoned-oil-and-gas-wells

**Date: 2026-05-07.** SPH Engineering's drone-based methane detection workflow was validated in a peer-reviewed *Sensors* paper covering surveys at abandoned oil and gas wells. The sensor stack: DJI Matrice 300 RTK platform, Pergam Laser Falcon methane sensor, SPH SkyHub onboard computer, and UgCS flight planning software.

Field application: surveys over abandoned wells near Houston, Alaska. The method identifies anomalous methane zones suitable for ground-team follow-up but does not assign emissions to specific source wells or quantify emission rates directly. Peer-reviewed publication of a vendor stack is uncommon for this segment.

## https://doodlelabs.com/blog/drone-dominance-at-scale-why-supply-chain-matters-more-than-specs/

**Date: 2026-05-07.** Doodle Labs argues that Drone Dominance program success depends as much on scalable, NDAA-compliant supply chains as on radio performance specifications. The post discloses specific production-side metrics: 200,000+ radios per year manufacturing capacity, week-scale lead times, and pricing below $1,000 per radio in early procurement phases.

Mesh Rider radios are positioned around common hardware with multi-band variants, Blue UAS approval, and NDAA compliance. The thesis frames supply chain as the binding constraint at Drone Dominance scale rather than spec-sheet differences between competing radios.
